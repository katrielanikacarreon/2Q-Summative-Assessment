Palawan Local Delicacies

Overview

Palawan isn’t just about stunning beaches and natural wonders — it’s also a paradise for food lovers. From fresh seafood to unique local delicacies, Palawan offers a rich culinary experience reflecting its diverse culture and natural resources.

Seafood Delights

Must Try

Grilled Fish & Seafood BBQ Freshly caught fish, squid, and shrimp grilled over charcoal, served with rice and dipping sauces.

Lato (Seaweed Salad) Crunchy seaweed salad seasoned with vinegar, tomatoes, and onions.

Crocodile Sisig Palawan’s crocodile meat sizzling with spices on a hot plate.

Traditional Palawan Dishes

Chicken Inato Grilled native chicken marinated with local herbs and spices.

Ginataang Isda Fish cooked in coconut milk with vegetables and spices.

Kinilaw na Isda Fresh fish marinated in vinegar, calamansi, onions, and chili.

Sweet Treats & Snacks

Cassava Cake Traditional Filipino cassava cakes with local coconut flavors.

Turon Sweet banana spring rolls, often with jackfruit, fried to golden perfection.

Local Fruits Ripe mangoes, coconuts, lanzones, and mangosteen are widely available.

Restaurants & Food Experiences

Puerto Princesa Baywalk & Night Market Street foods like grilled seafood, kwek-kwek (quail eggs), and local desserts.

El Nido & Coron Seafood Restaurants Feast on freshly grilled seafood while enjoying sunset views over the ocean.

Palawan Farm-to-Table Dining Restaurants offering dishes made entirely from local farms and fisheries.
