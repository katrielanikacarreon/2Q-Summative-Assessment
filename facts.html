Facts and Demographics

Geography & Nature: Palawan is known for its pristine beaches, dramatic limestone cliffs, lagoons, and rich marine biodiversity. Kayangan Lake and El Nido are among the most famous spots for travelers seeking natural beauty.

Culture: The indigenous groups, like the Tagbanua and Palaw’an, have rich traditions in weaving, music, and rituals. Art, crafts, and cultural performances are central to Palawan’s identity.

People & Languages: Home to over a million people, Palawan has a mix of Cuyunon, Tagbanua, Palaw’an, and other ethnic groups. Languages commonly spoken include Cuyunon, Tagalog, and English.

Festivals: The Baragatan Festival celebrates Palawan’s vibrant culture with street dancing, parades, and traditional music, highlighting the island’s rich heritage and community spirit.

Maritime Traditions: Sailing and fishing are part of Palawan’s history. Local boats called "paraws" showcase skillful craftsmanship and remain vital to the livelihood of many communities.
